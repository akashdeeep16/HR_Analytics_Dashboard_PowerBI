
# **💼 Cipla HR Analytics Dashboard**
### *An Interactive Workforce Analytics Solution for Employee Attrition, Demographics, and HR Performance Monitoring*
The Cipla HR Analytics Dashboard is a comprehensive Power BI solution designed to help HR professionals analyze workforce trends, monitor employee attrition, and evaluate demographic patterns across departments. The dashboard enables decision-makers to identify the key drivers of employee turnover, understand workforce composition, and make data-driven HR strategies to improve employee retention and organizational performance.

# **📌 Short Description / Purpose**
The Cipla HR Analytics Dashboard provides an interactive overview of the organization's workforce by tracking key HR metrics such as employee count, attrition rate, average employee tenure, salary distribution, education background, age demographics, and employee ratings.
The dashboard empowers HR managers and business leaders to identify high-risk employee groups, evaluate attrition trends, monitor workforce diversity, and develop effective retention strategies that support long-term organizational growth.

# **🛠 Tech Stack**
The dashboard was built using the following technologies:
* 📊 **Power BI Desktop** – Primary data visualization platform used for designing the interactive dashboard.
* 📂 **Power Query** – Used for data extraction, cleaning, transformation, and shaping before analysis.
* 🧠 **DAX (Data Analysis Expressions)** – Created KPIs, calculated measures, dynamic filtering, and percentage calculations.
* 🔗 **Data Modeling** – Established relationships between employee, department, education, and salary datasets for accurate reporting.
* 📈 **Interactive Visualizations** – Cards, Pie Charts, Donut Charts, Stacked Column Charts, Bar Charts, Matrix Tables, and Slicers.
* 📁 **File Formats**
  * `.pbix` – Dashboard development
  * `.png` – Dashboard preview
  * CSV – Source dataset
    
# **📂 Data Source**
**Source:** IBM HR Analytics Employee Attrition Dataset (Customized and presented as Cipla HR Analytics Dashboard)
The dataset contains detailed employee information including:
* Employee demographics
* Department
* Job Role
* Education
* Age
* Gender
* Salary Slab
* Years at Company
* Performance Rating
* Attrition Status
* Employee Count
The data was transformed and modeled to provide meaningful HR insights and interactive reporting.

# **✨ Features / Highlights**
## **📍 Business Problem**
Employee attrition is one of the biggest challenges faced by organizations because it increases recruitment costs, reduces productivity, and impacts overall business performance.
HR departments often struggle to answer questions like:
* Which employee groups have the highest attrition?
* Does salary influence employee turnover?
* Which education background experiences the highest attrition?
* Which age groups are leaving the organization most frequently?
* Which job roles require immediate retention strategies?
* How does employee experience vary across departments?
Without centralized analytics, answering these questions requires manual reporting and extensive data analysis.

# **🎯 Goal of the Dashboard**
The objective of this dashboard is to:
* Monitor overall workforce health.
* Measure employee attrition and retention.
* Identify departments and job roles with high employee turnover.
* Analyze workforce demographics.
* Support HR policy and retention planning.
* Enable leadership to make informed workforce decisions using interactive visual analytics.
  
# **📊 Walkthrough of Key Visuals**
## **1. KPI Summary Cards**
The dashboard begins with high-level HR KPIs that provide an instant overview of workforce performance.
Key metrics include:
* **Total Employees:** 1,470
* **Employees Left (Attrition):** 237
* **Attrition Rate:** 16.1%
* **Average Employee Tenure:** 7 Years
* **Active Employees:** 1,233
These KPIs help HR executives quickly monitor workforce stability.

## **2. Department Navigation Buttons**
Interactive navigation buttons allow users to switch between major business functions:
* Human Resources
* Research & Development
* Sales
This enables department-specific workforce analysis without creating separate dashboards.

## **3. Attrition by Education (Pie Chart)**
This visualization highlights employee attrition based on educational background.
It enables HR teams to determine which education groups experience the highest employee turnover and whether recruitment or retention strategies should be adjusted for specific educational segments.

## **4. Employee Distribution by Age Group and Gender (Stacked Column Chart)**
The stacked column chart displays workforce distribution across age groups while comparing male and female employees.
Age groups include:
* Under 25
* 26–35
* 36–45
* 46–55
* 55+
This visualization helps HR understand workforce demographics and gender balance across different career stages.

## **5. Employee Ratings by Job Role (Matrix Table)**
The matrix summarizes employee ratings across different job roles.
It enables managers to evaluate:
* Employee performance distribution
* High-performing job roles
* Roles with lower ratings
* Overall workforce quality
This helps identify departments that may require additional training or performance improvement initiatives.

## **6. Attrition by Salary (Horizontal Bar Chart)**
The salary analysis visual categorizes employee attrition across salary ranges:
* Up to 5K
* 5K–10K
* 10K–15K
* Above 15K
The chart clearly shows that employees in lower salary brackets contribute the highest proportion of attrition, helping HR evaluate compensation-related retention issues.

## **7. Workforce Distribution by Age Group (Donut Charts)**
Individual donut charts display the gender composition for each age category:
* Under 25
* 26–35
* 36–45
* 46–55
* 55+
These visuals help HR understand demographic diversity within each age segment and support workforce planning.

# **📈 Business Impact & Insights**
### **Employee Retention Strategy**
The dashboard identifies employee groups with higher attrition, enabling HR teams to implement targeted retention initiatives.

### **Compensation Analysis**
Salary-based attrition highlights whether compensation packages are contributing to employee turnover and helps management review salary structures.

### **Workforce Planning**
Age and gender analysis assists HR in succession planning, talent acquisition, and future workforce forecasting.

### **Department Performance Monitoring**
Department-level navigation allows leadership to compare HR metrics across different business functions and identify operational challenges.

### **Talent Management**
Job role performance ratings help managers recognize high-performing teams while identifying roles that may require additional coaching or skill development.

### **Data-Driven HR Decision Making**
Instead of relying on static reports, HR leaders can interactively explore employee data to uncover trends, monitor organizational health, and make strategic workforce decisions.

# **🔍 Key Insights**
* The organization has **1,470 employees**, with **1,233 active employees** and an **attrition rate of 16.1%**.
* Employees earning **up to ₹5K** account for the highest level of attrition, indicating salary may be a significant retention factor.
* The **26–35** and **36–45** age groups represent the largest share of the workforce, making them critical segments for retention and career development initiatives.
* Attrition varies across different education backgrounds, highlighting opportunities for targeted employee engagement and learning programs.
* Job-role rating analysis helps identify teams with strong performance as well as areas where additional training and support may improve employee outcomes.
* The dashboard enables HR leaders to monitor workforce trends in real time, supporting proactive and evidence-based decision-making across the organization.
